Propagation

The following methods depend on the plant family, which categorises the way they can propagate.


Tools Needed

  • Pruning Shears or knives: Can be used to separate the plant into individual sections.
  • Rooting Hormone: Helps with more chance of root development.
  • Containers or Pot: For any of the methods involved separation to create separate for growth
    •  

    Methods

     

    Cuttings

    This involves taking a part of the plant (stem, leaf, or root), depending on the family, which can then be placed in water/soil to grow a new version. Commonly use perlite, moss or water to place the cutting.

    There is an big difference how it can be done between succulent and monstera.

    Division

    Divide the plant into two or more sections, each with its own root system, as some types don't have the ability to make a new one.

    Offset

    Plants that produce offshoots around the base of the parent plant can be gently separated in order to give the existing plant more space or create the same version.

    Layering

    Involves placing a stem or branch to produce roots without any separation when still attached to the plant; when noticed, the root can be started. This makes it very gentle for the beginners, for example, ivy, to utility without the risk of it dying. Use a holding pin to place the branch in place under the soil.
